Outdoor stuff

Baxter State Park, ME

June 2016

Baxter state park in Maine is most famous for Mt. Katahdin, the terminus of the Appalacian trail. It has some extremely challenging hikes like the Knife Edge, a scenic ridge trail, and the Cathedral trail, which I hear is basically rock climbing. Katahdin is gorgeous and should be on the bucket-list of any outdoor enthusiast, but we've discovered other parts of Baxter which are very much also worth visiting, including our favorite campsite all time at South Branch Pond. Here's what we did while we were there.

Hiking Katahdin

We had a pleasant overnight stay the night before visiting Baxter at the quaint Parks Edge Inn, in Millinocket ME. The next morning we were at the entrace to Baxter very early. I don't remember exactly how early but I think it was definitely earlier than 6:30am. We arrived and hiked up to Chimney Pond, which takes about two hours. We had a reservation that night at Chimney Pond, but couldn't leave our stuff because it was occupied still in the morning when we passed by. If we had planned our trip earlier we would prefer to reserve two nights, but this worked out just fine.

The Knife's edge was closed and we didn't want to hike Cathedral with all our camping gear, so we opted for the Saddle trail on the way up, and Hamlin Ridge on the way down. This is still a strenuous hike, especially with all your gear. The summit is spectacular. Hamlin Ridge is very underrated! It is a longer journey than Saddle and seems to be less traveled, but the ridgeline views make it a great choice for the hike down. The total mileage for the day was 9 miles. With the crazy early wake-up, we went at a reasonable pace, took breaks as desired, and arrived to Chimney Pond comfortably at early dinner time. What a gorgeous camping area. The lean-to's are very nice.

South Branch Pond

As stunning as Katahdin is, the highlight of Baxter State Park for us was camping at South Branch pond. The northern part of the park is very quiet compared to Katahdin, and there was little competition for campsite reservations. There are some very pretty lean-to's right on the pond for car camping, but our absolute favorite campsite anywhere all time is the hike-in/canoe-in lean-to maybe a half mile from the main campgrounds. There is a very reasonable honor-code fee to reserve canoes at the campground. You can then throw all your things in a boat and paddle up to the site. Complete privacy. The site itself and the views of the Traveler loop ridgeline are idyllic.

There is plenty to entertain you on this pond if you rent a canoe. There is a ledge for cliff jumping (nothing too crazy), there is a hike following Howe brook which feeds the pond, along which there are cold refreshing pools forming under small waterfalls that are perfect for swimming. Some lakes in Baxter do have leeches but South Branch is not one of them. There is a gentle hiking trail that circumnavigates the pond, and if you are more ambitious, there is the infamous Traveler Loop, which some say is more strenuous than the Katahdin hikes. The 10 mile ridgeline hike is recommended for the fall because of intense heat and difficulty, so we did not attempt it during our June trip. We look forward to the opportunity to return, spend more time at our favorite South Branch lean-to, and take on the Traveler Loop.
