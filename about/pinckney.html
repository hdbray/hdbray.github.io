Outdoor stuff

Pinckney Recreation Area, MI

Pinckney is only a 45 minute drive from Ann Arbor but doesn't feel like it. There is a lot of hiking but this is probably better for mountain bikers and paddlers. Make sure you have a rec pass or buy a day pass at Halfmoon lake or Silver lake. We took our canoe out to the chain of lakes several times. Our friends rented kayaks with Hell Canoe and Kayak with put-in at Bruin lake campground, which is a great starting point. Paddled from here up to Watson, Patterson, and then Woodburn lake. We went up Portage creek, paddled upstream without effort, and weaved through the marshy area until the tree line (at which point the mosquitos came out and drove us to turn around). Next time we'll have to check out the other creek feeding Woodburn lake. The backcountry campground at Blind lake is a lovely space to stop. Sites at only reservable for one night at a time so it should be a quiet place to pull up for lunch. The channel between Hi-land lake and Halfmoon lake is very peaceful and pretty. Lots of fishing boats out and about. Go early in the AM to beat the traffic and avoid dealing with a lot of wake from motor boats. We hope to try paddling from Hi-land lake to Portage lake sometime, along a longer stretch of creek which I know nothing about. Might self-shuttle with my bike.
